# Equity Valuation and IPO Analysis

## Why this matters

Bonds promise contractual cash flows; equity receives uncertain residual cash flows. This lesson uses **Apex Technologies** as a decision context. The goal is not merely to reproduce a formula; it is to explain what the result means for a financial decision and what evidence could change the conclusion.

## Learning objectives

- Use dividend-based valuation
- Interpret relative valuation multiples
- Test IPO pricing scenarios

## The equity claim

### Residual ownership

Equity has no maturity and no guaranteed payout. Its value depends on future distributions, growth opportunities, and the risk of those outcomes.

### Return sources

Dividends and repurchases return cash, while price appreciation reflects changing expectations about future cash flow and required return.

### Growth must earn

Retaining more earnings does not automatically increase value. Growth adds value when reinvested capital earns more than the return investors require.

## Valuation approaches

### Dividend discount

The constant-growth model is useful for stable firms when growth is below the required return. Its output is highly sensitive to the spread between those two rates.

### Market multiples

P/E compares equity value with earnings; EV/EBITDA compares enterprise value with a pre-financing operating measure. Match numerator and denominator claims.

## IPO judgment

### Offer price

Underpricing may support aftermarket performance but transfers value from existing owners to new investors. Overpricing risks weak demand and reputation damage.

### Dilution

Analysts must use post-offering diluted shares and trace how proceeds affect cash, debt, investment, and future earnings.

### Scenario discipline

A single point estimate hides uncertainty. Base, downside, and upside cases reveal which assumptions truly drive the recommendation.

## Worked example

**Excel:** `=next_dividend/(required_return-growth_rate)`

**Manual logic:** $2.40 ÷ (10% − 4%)

**Expected conclusion:** $40.00 intrinsic value.

Stress that required return must exceed perpetual growth. The Apex workbook adds multiples and scenario analysis to avoid relying on one method.

## FactSet application

Before using a FactSet value, record the field name, company, fiscal period, units, currency, and retrieval date. Reconcile material inputs to the company filing or FactSet definition. A correct-looking formula built from mismatched periods or units is not a valid analysis.

## Decision questions

1. When is P/E a misleading comparison?
1. Who benefits and who loses when an IPO is intentionally underpriced?

## Key takeaways

- Equity is a residual claim.
- Match valuation methods to claim definitions.
- Use scenarios instead of false precision.
