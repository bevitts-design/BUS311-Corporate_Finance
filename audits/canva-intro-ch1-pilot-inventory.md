## Pilot visual standard

The pilot uses the BUS209 FactSet HTML decks as the approved visual reference while preserving BUS311's maintained HTML architecture: 1920×1080 authored slides, a 24px minimum type floor, static semantic markup, an inlined no-Shadow-DOM `<deck-stage>`, and presenter notes. The visual layer uses BUS209's navy/steel/teal/gold language with restrained terra reserved for risk and negative-value cues. Geist supplies the clean sans-serif presentation voice, JetBrains Mono supports labels and formulas, and Instrument Serif remains available for limited numeric emphasis. Seven original, unbranded editorial images are stored locally and used as visual chapter breaks; all instructional text remains native HTML.

The July graphical-delivery revision replaces the deck's densest card and bullet patterns with editable visual systems: conversation and outcome orbits, skill and tool workflows, a project path, a company-selection funnel, a CFO responsibility wheel, history timelines, scenario sorting, entity branches, agency chains, trade-off balances, a governance image treatment, a scandal-to-policy response chain, and a SOX shield. These are native scoped HTML/CSS or inline SVG rather than flattened screenshots.

The maintained runtime standardizes `#slide-N` URL hashes, previous/next controls, a counter, fullscreen button and `F` shortcut, notes with `N`, click zones, and direct-link initialization. The original Canva design and supplied PPTX remain content and source-intent references, not runtime dependencies.

## Visual treatment policy

- **Keep as local images only after rights and resolution are confirmed:** school/course branding, original owned photography, readable approved application screenshots, and any original artwork supplied in a PPTX/PDF export.
- **Rebuild as editable HTML/SVG:** all titles, body text, grade charts, timelines, PEMDAS, process diagrams, org charts, project roadmaps, tables, decision flows, governance diagrams, and SOX summaries.
- **Do not copy into the public pilot:** personal/family photos, QR codes whose destinations cannot be confirmed, licensed/third-party logo collages, low-resolution screenshots containing important text, and any proprietary FactSet screen. FactSet is represented with editable instructions and an official support link.
- **External links retained:** FactSet academic support, SEC/PCAOB SOX references, and the source-linked Gary Gensler YouTube video.

## Source issues retained as review flags

- The source title is Spring 2026, but the maintained pilot is Fall 2026. Endicott's official calendar confirms classes begin September 1, the last class day is December 11, and final exams run December 14–18, 2026.
- No Fall 2026 BUS311 syllabus or Canvas calendar was available in the inspected workspace. Course-specific exam and project milestones therefore use planned semester weeks rather than invented exact dates; those weeks must be replaced when the governing schedule is available.
- The source chronology contains dates that may be disputed or mistyped, especially `1780 & 1997` for inflation-indexed debt. These are preserved for fidelity and visibly treated as review items.
- The CFO compensation and trend claims identify organizations but not a year or direct source URL.
- The incentive example switches between “percent of profit” and “percentage of price.” The pilot retains and calls attention to that distinction.
- The public pilot excludes personal images and third-party logo/artwork collages even where the Canva source includes license labels.
- The PPTX export contains many full-slide raster composites and small decorative icons. None were added to the public pilot because the instructional content remains clearer and more editable as HTML/CSS/SVG.
