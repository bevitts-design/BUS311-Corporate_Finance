Taxes change the cash that remains

Finance distinguishes the rate paid on total income from the rate applied to the next dollar earned.

Tax vocabulary

Marginal and effective rates answer different questions

Taxes
Marginal tax rate

The next dollar

The statutory rate applied to an additional dollar of taxable income.

Useful for forecasting how an incremental decision changes taxes.

Effective tax rate

The total result

Total tax expense divided by pretax income.

Useful for understanding the overall tax burden reported by the company.

The denominator matters

The average rate uses all income; the marginal rate uses the last dollar

Taxes
Average rate = Total tax ÷ Total income
Marginal rate = Tax on the next dollar

Decision rule

For a new project, use the marginal rate because the project creates incremental taxable income.

Do firms make strategic decisions based on cash flow?

Yes—but the decision depends on when cash arrives, how risky it is, and what alternatives are available.

Decision making

Discounted cash flow

Value is the present value of cash the asset can generate

Valuation
Value = Σ FCFt ÷ (1 + r)t

Forecast cash flow, choose the rate that matches its risk, then discount each future amount to today.

g

Growth

Changes in long-run growth can move value materially.

r

Discount rate

More risk raises the required return and reduces present value.

The fundamental theorem of finance

“The value of any asset is the present value of its future cash flows.”

Match cash flow to discount rate

Use the firm lens or the equity lens—then stay consistent

Appendix · Decision 1

Capital budgeting chooses long-term investments

Reference

Managers identify opportunities whose future cash flows are worth more than the resources required to acquire them.

Invest when value created > cost to acquire

Typical focus: property, plant, equipment, technology, acquisitions, and new products.

Appendix · Decision 2

Capital structure selects the mix of debt and equity

Reference
Debt

Contractual claim

Interest and repayment commitments can lower flexibility but may reduce the cost of capital.

Equity

Residual ownership

No promised repayment, but investors require compensation for greater risk.

The objective is not “least debt” or “most debt.” It is a financing mix that supports value and resilience.

Appendix · Tangible assets

Depreciation allocates cost; it does not use current-period cash

Reference

Wear, use, and obsolescence reduce a tangible asset’s accounting value over its useful life.

Depreciation is added back in OCF
