# Corporate Finance and Value Creation

## Why this matters

BUS210 measured money over time; BUS311 decides where corporate money should go. This lesson uses **Costco** as a decision context. The goal is not merely to reproduce a formula; it is to explain what the result means for a financial decision and what evidence could change the conclusion.

## Learning objectives

- Frame the financial manager’s three decisions
- Distinguish value creation from accounting profit
- Evaluate stakeholder trade-offs ethically

## The finance function

### Invest

Capital budgeting asks which long-lived investments deserve scarce funds. The relevant comparison is incremental future cash flow versus opportunity cost, not whether a proposal sounds exciting.

### Finance

Financing decisions affect risk, flexibility, taxes, and the claims different investors hold. There is no universally correct mix; the answer depends on the firm and its strategy.

### Distribute

Payout policy includes dividends and repurchases. A company should not retain cash merely to grow larger; retained funds should have a credible value-creating use.

## Value, cash flow, and incentives

### Cash beats labels

Accounting profit is necessary information but not the valuation endpoint. Finance translates operating performance into expected cash flows, timing, and risk.

### Opportunity cost

The cost of capital reflects what investors could earn on investments with comparable risk. A project creates value only when it clears that opportunity-cost hurdle.

## Governance and judgment

### Agency tension

Governance systems align managers with owners and other stakeholders through oversight, incentives, disclosure, and accountability. Incentives should reward durable outcomes rather than short-term optics.

### Stakeholders matter

Customers, employees, suppliers, communities, and creditors influence the firm’s ability to generate cash. Ignoring them can convert a short-term gain into a long-term liability.

### Decision standard

A professional finance recommendation names the decision, quantifies the value case, identifies key risks, and explains what evidence could change the conclusion.

## Worked example

**Excel:** `=NPV(discount_rate, future_cash_flows)+initial_outlay`

**Manual logic:** PV of benefits − required investment

**Expected conclusion:** Accept only when NPV is positive after risk-appropriate discounting.

Use this as a preview rather than a full NPV lesson. Emphasize that every later BUS311 model supports this same decision standard.

## Decision questions

1. When might maximizing this quarter’s earnings reduce long-run value?
1. Which Costco stakeholder relationship most directly supports durable cash flow?

## Key takeaways

- Finance allocates scarce capital.
- Value depends on cash, timing, and risk.
- Recommendations must explain trade-offs.
